## 1. Executive Summary (TL;DR)

This thesis is built on a single governing belief: **capital rotates when incentives change**, not when narratives change. The 2026 setup is defined by fiscal and tax incentives that primarily target **producers**—small businesses, manufacturers, and lenders—rather than consumers alone. If these incentives translate into observable increases in capex, lending, and production, market leadership should rotate away from mega-cap growth and toward **materials, industrials, regional banks, and domestically oriented cyclicals**.

The thesis explicitly ignores financial media narratives. Confirmation comes only from **price action, relative strength, credit conditions, labor data, and producer behavior**. If those data contradict the policy narrative, the thesis fails and positioning must adjust.

**Bull case:** Producer incentives work, capex accelerates, credit flows through regional banks, commodities remain firm, and small caps outperform through mid-terms.  
**Bear case:** Credit tightens, policy durability is questioned, consumer weakness overwhelms incentives, and the rotation stalls or reverses.

**Key pillars:**
1. Producer incentives > consumer stimulus  
2. Credit availability is the gating factor  
3. Materials and industrials lead cyclically  
4. Regional banks validate the transmission mechanism  
5. Data overrides narrative at all times

## 2. Policy Architecture Analysis

Policy is treated as a **conditional input**, not a certainty. Several provisions remain unverified and are therefore **non-actionable** until confirmed via primary sources.

### Consumer-Facing Provisions (All RESEARCH REQUIRED)
- No tax on tips  
- No tax on overtime  
- No tax on Social Security  
- Car loan interest deduction  
- Child Tax Credit adjustments  
- SALT cap increase  
- Credit card APR cap (CRITICAL RISK)  
- SNAP cuts  

**Assessment:** Consumer relief is mixed and fragile. Any credit rationing caused by an APR cap could offset headline tax benefits.

### Producer-Facing Provisions (Thesis-Critical)
- Section 179 expensing expansion  
- Small business deductions / QBI  
- Lender tax incentives  
- Infrastructure and industrial modernization  

**Assessment:** Producer provisions form the **core transmission mechanism**. Without observable increases in equipment orders and loan growth, the thesis fails.

All policy items remain **⚠️ POLICY DEPENDENT** pending verification.

- **Low-income households:** Net negative to neutral (transfer risk + credit tightening)
- **Middle-income households:** Net positive stabilizer
- **Small businesses:** High upside *if credit flows*
- **Large corporations:** Marginal benefit only

## 4. Housing Market Framework

Housing remains constrained by the **rate lock-in effect**:
- Large share of mortgages <4%
- Existing inventory suppressed
- New homes clear primarily through incentives

Speculative policy items (non-actionable):
- Fannie/Freddie purchase expansion
- 50-year mortgages

**Investment takeaway:** Housing supports industrial and logistics demand but is not the primary growth engine. Builder margins remain vulnerable.
