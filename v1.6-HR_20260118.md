## 1. Executive Summary (TL;DR)

This thesis is built on a single governing belief: **capital rotates when incentives change**, not when narratives change. The 2026 setup is defined by potential fiscal and tax incentives aimed at producers (small businesses, manufacturers, lenders) rather than consumers alone. If these incentives translate into real capex, lending, and production behavior, the market leadership should rotate away from mega-cap growth toward **materials, industrials, small caps, and regional banks**.

The thesis explicitly ignores financial media narratives. Confirmation comes only from **price action, relative strength, credit conditions, labor data, and producer behavior**. If these data contradict the policy narrative, the thesis fails and must be exited.

**Bull case:** Producer incentives work, capex accelerates, credit flows through regional banks, commodities remain firm, and small caps outperform through mid-terms.  
**Bear case:** Credit tightens, policy durability is questioned, consumer weakness overwhelms incentives, and the rotation stalls or reverses.

**Key pillars:**
1. Producer incentives > consumer stimulus  
2. Credit availability is the gating factor  
3. Materials and industrials lead cyclically  
4. Small caps confirm or deny the thesis  
5. Data overrides narrative at all times
